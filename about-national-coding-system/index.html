About

Background

The National Coding System (NCS) is Myanmar’s official coding for all the country’s administrative units. It is based on the eight (8) hierarchical levels of political units of the country, namely, the administrative region/state/Union territory, the district, the self-administered zone/division, the township, the town/city, the ward, the village tract and the village.All departments, ministries, and stakeholders shall use the NCS as the standard codes for administrative units. It is the backbone for interoperability. It allows linkages and exchange of data from different data sources and data providers. The NCS has been developed and agreed by the National Coding System Technical Working Group composed of:
  • General Administration Department (chair), Ministry of Office of the Union Government
  • Department of Population, , Ministry of Labour, Immigration and Population
  • Central Statistics Organisation, Ministry of Planning and Finance
  • Department of Rural Development, Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Irrigation
  • Survey Department, Ministry of Natural Resources and Environmental Conservation
  • Department of Agricultural Land Management and Statistics, Ministry of Agriculture, Livestock and Irrigation
  • Forestry Department, Ministry of Natural Resources and Environmental Conservation
  • Department of Basic Education, Ministry of Education
  • Department of Public Health, Ministry of Health and Sports The Myanmar Information Management Unit (MIMU) and OneMap Myanmar provided technical support to the initiative.
The General Administration Department (GAD) of the Ministry of the Office of the Union Government is the curator of Myanmar’s NCS and as such maintains and updates it. It ensures the NCS is available to all users in user-friendly, readily accessible formats and platforms in a timely manner.

NCS Structure

National coding system has eight Administrative structure as follow
  • Union Territory, State/Region
  • District
  • Township
  • City, Town
  • Ward
  • Village Tract
  • Village
  • Self Administered Zone

Code format

Code format: GALdddddd

GA = brand code for “General Administration Department” to distinguish from any other codes produced by other organizations

L = Admin level 1 to 8

dddddd = six unique digits
